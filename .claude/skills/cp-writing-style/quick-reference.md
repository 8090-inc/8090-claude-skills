## The 11 Commandments (One-Line Version)

1. **Radical Clarity**: Say it simpler
2. **Data Over Adjectives**: Show numbers, not descriptions
3. **Honesty First**: Acknowledge failures upfront
4. **First-Principles**: Explain from fundamentals
5. **Economy**: Cut every wasted word
6. **Active Voice**: People do things
7. **Context**: Always explain "why"
8. **Attribution**: Credit sources
9. **Forward-Looking**: Point to next, drive outcomes
10. **Format Function**: Structure serves message
11. **Strategic Authenticity (RIZZ)**: Deploy charisma through vulnerability, energy, and intellectual flex

## Banned vs. Golden

### ‚ùå Banned Words/Phrases

**Vague Adjectives**:
- Significant, substantial, considerable
- Very, really, quite, actually

**Corporate Jargon**:
- Leverage, synergy, paradigm
- Going forward, moving forward
- Drive toward, laser-focused, de-risk
- Socialize, alignment, stakeholder engagement
- Stand up, operationalize, at scale
- Touch base, circle back, shift right

**Euphemisms**:
- Challenges (say "problems")
- Opportunities for improvement (say "failures")
- Rightsizing (say "layoffs")

### ‚úÖ Golden Phrases

**Transitions**:
- "What happened?"
- "This resulted in..."
- "What does this mean for..."
- "Put more simply, X > Y"
- "The reason lies with..."

**Causal**:
- "Because [reason], [action]"
- "Since [context], [implication]"

**Analysis**:
- "Conventionally, [X]... However, [Y]"
- "Throughout [time period], [observation]"

**Directives**:
- "Run it by [name] before you send it pls"
- "You need to..."

## Universal Editing Checklist

Use for ALL formats:

### Content
- [ ] Specific numbers, not adjectives?
- [ ] Failures acknowledged upfront?
- [ ] First-person accountability ("I/we")?
- [ ] Explained "why" for every "what"?
- [ ] Active voice (not passive)?
- [ ] Sources attributed?

### Language
- [ ] Could cut 20% more words?
- [ ] No banned phrases?
- [ ] No hedging ("possibly," "maybe")?
- [ ] Technical terms defined on first use?
- [ ] Reading level ~10th grade?

### Structure
- [ ] Most important information first?
- [ ] Headers signal topic shifts?
- [ ] Bullets for parallel items, numbers for sequential?
- [ ] Tables for comparative data?

### Next Steps
- [ ] Forward-looking (what happens next)?
- [ ] Clear ownership assigned?
- [ ] Specific timelines/dates?

## Decision Trees

### "Should I use bullets or prose?"

```
Is this a Policy Ideas Brief?
  YES ‚Üí Use bullets (70-80% bullets)
  NO ‚Üì

Is this a list of parallel items or action steps?
  YES ‚Üí Use bullets
  NO ‚Üì

Is this explaining causal relationships or building an argument?
  YES ‚Üí Use narrative prose
  NO ‚Üì

DEFAULT ‚Üí Use narrative prose
```

### "Pattern 1 or Pattern 2 for Customer Brief?"

```
Multiple projects needing comparison?
  YES ‚Üì

Reader already has context?
  YES ‚Üì

Time-constrained (< 5 min)?
  YES ‚Üí Pattern 2 (Decision-Driven: table-first)
  NO ‚Üí Pattern 1 (Strategic Hook: narrative-first)
```

### "Should I cut more words?"

```
After first draft: Cut 50%
After second draft: Cut 30% more
After third draft: If uncertain, cut 20% more

Target reductions:
- Corporate jargon ‚Üí 240 words to 47 words (80%)
- Email ‚Üí Always under 200 words
- Customer brief ‚Üí 50% reduction typical
```

## RIZZ CHEATSHEET (Principle 11)

**For full deep-dive**: Load `advanced-rhetoric.md`

### Energy Modulation Matrix

| Purpose | Energy Level | When to Use | Example Phrases |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Celebration** | 8-10/10 | Team wins, deals closed, hiring success | "FIRST week where I felt we executed well," "PETE DOWNEY!!!!" |
| **Correction** | 8-9/10 | Direct feedback, urgent fixes | "You suck at X. Do Y instead." |
| **Teaching** | 4-6/10 | Philosophical lessons, frameworks | "The distance between us and success is ~1M errors" |
| **Gratitude** | 5-7/10 | Acknowledging team effort | "I really appreciate all of you" |
| **Vulnerability** | 3-5/10 | Admitting mistakes, apologizing | "I should have done a better job... I will try to do better" |

### Selective Vulnerability Strategy

**What to Admit** (Strategic):
- ‚úÖ Past financial errors with specific numbers: "$3-4B Bitcoin mistake"
- ‚úÖ Intellectual evolution: "I was wrong about Trump"
- ‚úÖ Personal struggles: "I'm just tired and miss my kids"
- ‚úÖ Process failures: "should have done better job of updating you"

**What to Avoid** (Defensive):
- ‚ùå Real-time contradictions (don't admit error while making same error)
- ‚ùå Generic apologies ("mistakes were made")
- ‚ùå Deflecting power/privilege

**Pattern**: Pre-emptive vulnerability ‚Üí permission for confidence

### Metaphor Systems Library

| Domain | Use Case | CP Example |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Books/Chapters** | Company evolution stages | "Prologue ‚Üí Introduction ‚Üí Chapter 1" |
| **Sports** | Team building, execution | "Fundamentals," "buying ticket to rollercoaster" |
| **Science** | Processes, constraints | "Forcing functions," "boundary conditions" |
| **Journey/Phases** | Long-term vision | "SpaceX Phase 1 (8 yrs) ‚Üí Phase 2 (10+ yrs) ‚Üí Phase 3" |

### Feedback Patterns

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Direct Correction** | "[Problem]. [Command]. [Reason]." | "You suck at politics. Escalate to me and get back to your strengths." |
| **Constructive** | "[What didn't work] because [why]. Solution: [action]." | "Team did poor job managing this. We'll meet late in week to review priorities." |
| **Appreciative** | "[Name]'s [specific action] [why it matters]" | "I appreciated Andrew and Vivian's ownership of their role" |

### Intellectual Flex (Show, Don't Tell)

**Tactics**:
- üéØ **Data Fluency**: Drop specific numbers casually ("$150B AI investment," "Fed cut from 5.33% to 4.75%")
- üìö **"Learn with Me"**: Frame as learning journey ("I was looking at failure modes in startups last night")
- ‚ùì **Valorize "I don't know"**: Makes actual knowledge claims more credible
- üó£Ô∏è **Plain Language + Density**: Complex ideas in simple words

**What NOT to do**: "I've studied this extensively," "As an expert," "I know a lot about..."

### Contrarian Positioning Formula

**When to Use**: Public thought leadership, building personal brand

**Formula**: [Elite Credential] + [Insider Experience] + [Public Rejection of Consensus] = Authority + Authenticity

**Examples**:
- Former Facebook VP critiques social media
- VC calls VCs "soulless cowards"
- Elite education holder says "college was exact opposite of good advice"

**Risk**: Must pre-empt hypocrisy with Principle 3 (Honesty First)

### Debate Tactics (Quick Ref)

**For high-stakes public communications only**:

1. **Socratic Hammer**: Repeated questioning to expose contradictions ("Who says that? Who says that?")
2. **Rephrasing Trap**: "That's what you're saying. You may not know that's what you're saying, but that's what you're saying."
3. **Data Asymmetry**: Provide specific numbers, challenge opponent's vague claims
4. **False Binary**: "Either rise to meet the moment or disappear"

### Zinger Construction

**5-Element Formula**:
1. Binary/contrastive structure ("X > Y," "X vs. Y")
2. Name enemies specifically ("soulless cowards" not "some people")
3. Unexpected insider attack (attack own class/industry)
4. Specific numbers ("$3-4B mistake" not "big mistake")
5. Emotional honesty (raw feeling words: "vacant and empty," "pain and suffering")

**Example**: "Often wrong, never in doubt" (binary, 4 words, memorable)

### RIZZ Context Calibration

| Context | RIZZ Components to Use | Skip |
|---------|------------------------|------|
| **Internal team emails** | Energy modulation, vulnerability, metaphors | Contrarian positioning, debate tactics |
| **External partners** | Intellectual flex, measured energy | Vulnerability, debate tactics |
| **Public/investors** | FULL RIZZ (all components) | None |
| **Tactical emails** | None (pure efficiency) | All |

### RIZZ Quality Check

Before sending high-stakes communication:
- [ ] Energy level matches purpose (celebrate/correct/teach)?
- [ ] Vulnerability strategic (specific numbers, not generic)?
- [ ] Metaphor system sustained throughout (if teaching)?
- [ ] At least one quotable zinger (binary + specific + emotional)?
- [ ] Intellectual flex demonstrated through deployment, not declaration?

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "We saw significant growth" | "Revenue grew 47% QoQ from $2.1M to $3.1M" |
| "Mistakes were made" | "We made mistakes. I learned from them." |
| "Despite some challenges..." | State the challenge first with data, then wins |
| Email > 200 words | Split into multiple emails, one per topic |
| "Over the next 100 days" | Use specific days: "Over the next 80 days" |
| Missing "why" | Add: "Because [reason], [action]" |
| Passive voice | "We did X" not "X was done" |
| Table without context | Add: "Calculated using [method] assuming [assumptions]" |

## The 30-Second Test

Before sending any communication, ask:

1. **Purpose**: Can I state the single purpose in one sentence?
2. **Data**: Did I replace adjectives with numbers?
3. **Honesty**: Did I acknowledge failures first?
4. **Brevity**: Could I cut 20% more without losing meaning?
5. **Action**: Is it clear what happens next?

If any answer is "NO," revise before sending.
